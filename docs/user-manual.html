Introduction
Getting Started
About RetailERP

RetailERP (EL CURIO) is a cloud-based retail enterprise resource planning platform designed for manufacturers and distributors of footwear, bags, belts, and leather goods. It provides end-to-end coverage from product catalog management through inventory, ordering, production, billing, and analytics — all in a single, integrated system.

1.1 Logging In

Open a supported browser (Google Chrome recommended) and navigate to your organization's RetailERP URL (e.g., https://erp.elcurio.com or the address provided by your administrator).

Login Procedure

  1. On the login page, enter your Email address in the email field.
  2. Enter your Password in the password field. Click the eye icon to toggle visibility.
  3. Click the Sign In button. If credentials are valid, you are redirected to the Dashboard.
  4. On your first login, you will be prompted to change your temporary password. Enter and confirm your new password, then click Update Password.
⚠
Security Notice
Change the default password immediately after first login. Use a password of at least 8 characters containing uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and a special character. Never share your login credentials.

1.4 Common Operations

Search, Filter & Sort

Every list page includes a search bar that filters by the primary identifier fields of that module. Additional filter dropdowns, date range pickers, and toggle switches appear above the table. Filters apply immediately. Click Clear Filters to reset. Column headers can be clicked to sort ascending/descending.

Pagination

Large datasets are paginated. Default page size is 10 rows; change via the page-size selector to 10, 25, 50, or 100 rows. Navigate with Previous / Next buttons or click a specific page number. The total count is shown ("Showing 1–10 of 247 records").

Export to CSV

  1. Apply any desired filters to narrow the data set.
  2. Click the Download / Export CSV button (top-right of the list).
  3. The browser downloads a CSV file named with the module and date (e.g., Invoices-2026-03-22.csv).
  4. Open in Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets for further analysis.

CRUD Pattern

All master and transactional modules follow the same create/read/update/delete pattern:

  • Create — Click + Add [Record] button (top-right). A modal or full-page form opens.
  • View — Click the row or the eye icon to open the detail view.
  • Edit — Click the pencil icon on the row. The form opens pre-filled with current values.
  • Delete — Click the trash icon. A confirmation dialog appears before deletion. Records with dependencies cannot be hard-deleted.
💡
Tip: Soft Delete vs. Hard Delete
Most records support "soft delete" — the record is deactivated rather than permanently removed, preserving historical data integrity. To truly remove a record, all dependent child records must be reassigned or deleted first. When in doubt, deactivate rather than delete.

Chapter 2
Master Data Management
About Master Data

The Masters module contains all reference lookup tables that the rest of the system depends on. These must be populated before creating articles, placing orders, or generating invoices. Set up master data in the sequence shown below, as some items depend on others (e.g., Sub-Segments require Segments to exist first).

📋
Recommended Setup Sequence
1. Brands → 2. Genders → 3. Seasons → 4. Segments → 5. Sub-Segments → 6. Categories → 7. Sub-Categories → 8. Groups → 9. Sizes → 10. Articles → 11. SKUs

2.1 Brands

Dashboard › Masters › Brands

Brands represent the product brand labels under which your articles are marketed. Each article must be associated with exactly one brand.

Field Descriptions

FieldTypeRequiredDescription

How to Add a Brand

  1. Navigate to Dashboard > Masters > Brands.
  2. Click the + Add Brand button in the top-right corner.
  3. Enter the Brand Name (e.g., "LeatherLux").
  4. Ensure the Active toggle is ON (default).
  5. Click Save. The modal closes and the brand list refreshes.

How to Edit a Brand

  1. Locate the brand in the list using the search bar or by scrolling.
  2. Click the pencil (Edit) icon on the brand's row.
  3. Modify the Brand Name or toggle Active status.
  4. Click Save to apply changes.

How to Delete a Brand

  1. Click the trash (Delete) icon on the brand's row.
  2. A confirmation dialog appears. Click Confirm to proceed.
⚠
Constraint
Deletion fails if any articles reference the brand. Reassign or deactivate dependent articles first, or set the brand's Active status to OFF instead of deleting.

2.3 Segments & Sub-Segments

Dashboard › Masters › Segments  |  Sub-Segments

Segments represent the primary product line divisions (e.g., Footwear, Leather Goods). They are the broadest classification and drive attribute display, reporting, and invoice formatting. Sub-Segments provide a second tier within a segment (e.g., Formal, Casual, Sports under Footwear; Travel, Fashion under Leather Goods).

FieldTypeRequiredDescription
Segment NameTextYesProduct line name (e.g., "Footwear", "Leather Goods"). Must be unique.

ℹ
Segment vs. Category
Segment = product line / business division (e.g., "Footwear"). Category = physical product type (e.g., "Shoes"). An article belongs to both — for example, a Derby shoe is in the "Footwear" segment and the "Shoes" category.

How to Add SKUs to an Article

  1. Open the article in edit mode (pencil icon on the article row).
  2. Navigate to the Sizes / SKUs tab or section within the article form.
  3. Click + Add Size.
  4. Select the Euro Size from the size chart dropdown. UK, US, and Indian sizes are auto-populated.
  5. Enter a size-specific MRP if it differs from the article MRP.
  6. Click Save. The EAN code and barcode are generated automatically.
  7. Repeat for all applicable sizes in the article's size run.

Barcode Labels

Dashboard › Masters › Barcode Labels

The Barcode Labels module allows printing of barcode labels for individual SKUs or batch printing for a set of articles/sizes.

  1. Navigate to Dashboard > Masters > Barcode Labels.
  2. Search and select the articles/SKUs you want to print labels for.
  3. Set the quantity per label (how many copies of each barcode to print).
  4. Select the label format (size in mm, layout).
  5. Click Print or Download PDF. Labels are formatted for standard label sheets.
💡
Tip: Batch Label Printing
For new season launches, use the batch print feature — filter by Season and Segment, select all matching articles, and print labels for all SKUs at once. This saves significant time when labeling a new collection.

GST Pricing Formula

EL CURIO Margin Formula
ON MRP margin type:
  Unit Price = MRP × (1 − Margin%)
  Taxable Amount = Unit Price × Quantity
  IGST (inter-state) = Taxable Amount × 18%
  CGST + SGST (intra-state) = Taxable Amount × 9% each

NET OF TAXES margin type:
  Unit Price = MRP × (1 − Margin%) ÷ (1 + GST Rate)
  Taxable Amount = Unit Price × Quantity
  GST = Taxable Amount × GST Rate

Appendix D
Glossary

Article

A unique product style and color combination in the catalog. The central product master record in RetailERP.

SKU

Stock Keeping Unit — the most granular product level. Each SKU is a unique Article + Size combination with its own barcode.

GRN

Goods Receipt Note — a document recording goods received into a warehouse. Creates an inward stock movement.

BOM

Bill of Materials — the complete list of raw materials and quantities required to manufacture one unit of a finished article.

MRP

Maximum Retail Price — the highest price at which an article can be legally sold to a consumer. Basis for all margin calculations.

CBD

Cost Breakdown — the internal manufacturing or procurement cost per article. Used for margin and profitability analysis.

SOR

Sale or Return — a consignment business model where goods are supplied to a retailer and unsold stock can be returned.

OUT_MKT

Outright Market — a firm sale model where goods are sold and cannot be returned except for valid reasons.

MBO

Multi-Brand Outlet — a retail store that carries products from multiple brands.

EBO

Exclusive Brand Outlet — a retail store dedicated exclusively to one brand.

Segment

The primary product line division (e.g., Footwear, Leather Goods). Drives attribute display and reporting.

Category

The physical product type classification (e.g., Shoes, Bags, Belts). Second-level classification after Segment.

Place of Supply

The buyer's state of registration. Determines whether CGST+SGST (intra-state) or IGST (inter-state) applies on an invoice.

Credit Note

A document issued to reduce the amount owed by a customer — typically for goods returned or prices adjusted downward.

Debit Note

A document issued to increase the amount owed by a customer — for additional charges or upward price adjustments.

Stock Freeze

A period-end operation that locks stock quantities as of a specific date, creating closing stock for one period and opening stock for the next.

RBAC

Role-Based Access Control — a security model where users are assigned roles, and roles have defined permissions across modules.

UOM

Unit of Measure — how quantities are counted (e.g., PAIRS for footwear, PCS for bags and belts).

Appendix E
Frequently Asked Questions

General

Q: I forgot my password. How do I reset it?
A: Contact your system administrator. They can reset your password from Admin > Users by entering a new password for your account. There is no self-service password reset at this time.

Q: Can multiple people use the system simultaneously?
A: Yes. RetailERP supports concurrent users. Each session is independent, and all changes are committed to the central database in real-time.

Q: What browsers are supported?
A: Google Chrome (recommended), Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, and Safari. Internet Explorer is not supported.

Q: Is there a mobile app?
A: RetailERP is a responsive web application that works on mobile browsers. There is no separate native app. For best mobile experience, use Chrome or Safari and add the app to your home screen.

Master Data

Q: I cannot delete a brand. Why?
A: Master records referenced by other records (articles, orders, invoices) cannot be deleted to preserve data integrity. Deactivate the brand instead by setting its Active status to OFF.

Q: What is the difference between Segment and Category?
A: Segment = product line / business division (Footwear, Leather Goods). Category = physical product type (Shoes, Bags, Belts). An article belongs to both.

Q: In what order should I set up master data?
A: Follow this sequence: (1) Brands → (2) Genders → (3) Seasons → (4) Segments → (5) Sub-Segments → (6) Categories → (7) Sub-Categories → (8) Groups → (9) Sizes → (10) Articles → (11) SKUs

Inventory

Q: What does "Frozen" stock mean?
A: Frozen stock has been locked for period-end closing. No transactions (receipt, dispatch, return, adjustment) can be posted against frozen stock until it is unfrozen by an Admin or Manager.

Q: Why can't I dispatch more than the available quantity?
A: The system enforces real-time stock availability checks — this is a hard constraint. If you need to dispatch more, first record a stock receipt (GRN) to bring in additional stock.

Q: How does the monthly stock ledger work?
A: The formula is: Opening Stock + All Inward Movements − All Outward Movements = Closing Stock. When stock is frozen at month-end, the Closing Stock becomes the next month's Opening Stock.

Orders & Billing

Q: Can I edit a confirmed order?
A: No. Once confirmed, an order is locked. To make changes, cancel the order (providing a reason) and create a new corrected order.

Q: How does the system determine CGST+SGST vs. IGST?
A: Based on Place of Supply: If your company's state equals the buyer store's state → CGST (9%) + SGST (9%). If different states → IGST (18%).

Q: What is the difference between NET OF TAXES and ON MRP margin types?
A: ON MRP: discount calculated directly on MRP. NET OF TAXES: discount calculated on the MRP excluding the tax component, resulting in a slightly different unit price. Your client agreement determines which to use.

Q: Can I export reports to Excel?
A: Reports export as CSV files, which open natively in Microsoft Excel and Google Sheets. Click the Download / Export CSV button on any report page.

Administration

Q: What happens when I deactivate a user?
A: Deactivated users cannot log in. Their active sessions are terminated immediately. All historical records they created (orders, invoices, audit entries) are preserved unchanged.

Q: Can I create custom roles?
A: The four built-in roles (Admin, Storemanager, Accountuser, Viewer) cover most scenarios. You cannot create entirely new role names, but you can customize the permissions within each role via the Roles and Permissions matrix.

Q: How do I back up system data?
A: RetailERP uses a SQL Server database backend. Contact your system administrator or IT team to configure regular database backups. The application does not provide a built-in backup UI.
